Character Ideas

  • Briefly share character ideas and backgrounds we have so far
  • Stats will be rolled later

Fantasy Genre

  • What aspects of the fantasy genre are you interested in?
  • What kind of adventures do you want to go on, thematically (e.g. pirates / jungle temples / ...)
  • What fantasy films or books do you like, and what similarities would you like to see in this game?

Tone & Safety

  • As a rule of thumb, if this game were a film it would be rated 15, i.e. themes like sex or torture should be avoided or at least happen 'off screen'.
  • If there are any themes you don't want to see in the game, let me know (privately or anonymously is fine, if you prefer)
  • Ask for a pause if you are finding a scene uncomfortable or worry that someone else might be. We can do a script change to ensure everyone's enjoying the game

Staying In Character

Use appropriate words

  • Address each other by character names
  • Avoid terms that wouldn't make sense to the characters, e.g: "spell slots", "AOE", "2d6", "hit points", etc.
  • Try to narrate some of your character's actions ("I check for tracks in the dirt or freshly broken plant stems" is preferrable to "I roll my +3 Survival skill to look for clues")

Discussions

  • What you say at the table is what your characters are saying out loud (unless it's obviously describing an action: "I climb the ladder", or a rules question)
  • Nearby NPCs and monsters might overhear you, and conversely if characters are positioned far apart you might not hear each other
  • Time passes while you discuss things - both in and out of combat - so strategic planning mid-combat should be kept to a few 'free action' words

EncourageRefocusing

Jokes, phone notifications, and general chaos is bound to break the flow sometimes - and that's fine - but try to refocus quickly and prompt others to do so too if needed

MiscStuff

  • You're welcome to do accents and/or acting if you want but your don't have to
  • You don't have to be eloquent and silver-tongued in the real world to play a character with those traits (just like you don't have to be muscular or able to do magic in the real world to play that kind of class!)
  • Say something like "pause" or "time-out" if you want a brief stop, e.g. to check a rule
  • Anyone can call for a longer break at any time

House Rules

  • Floor dice don’t count. No exceptions.
  • Your alignment doesn't inform your play style, rather your play style informs your alignment
    • Periodically, as a group, we will discuss and choose an alignment for each character based on their recent actions
    • NB: Some class abilities and magic items may be affected by your alignment. The rule text will say so if this is the case.
  • You can have a do-over on what your character says or does if only a few seconds have gone by and no new information has been revealed
  • Players who take part in the recap will start the session with inspiration. Also if I forget to award inspiration when something suitably dramatic happens then please request it!
  • Rule of cool will generally win out over rules as written, but don’t expect consistency. The GM reserves their right to be fickle.
  • Feel free to make any reasonable assumptions about the people and objects in the scene
    • e.g. At a harbour you don't need to ask "Are there any crates or barrels nearby?", just proceed as if there are and we'll all mentally add them to the scene.
  • If you want to push your luck with a less plausable but not game-breaking change to the scene you can use your inspiration to request it. These should make for a cool moment but not feel 'cheaty' or make a challenge unreasonably easy. e.g:
    Suitable examples:
    • "It suddenly starts raining"
    • "There's a chandelier which I swing from as I leap from the balcony"
    Not gonna happen:
    • "I find a chest containing 10,000 gold pieces at my feet"
    • "An ancient bronze dragon appears and fights at my side"
    NB: either way, DM veto applies!

World Building

'World' doesn't necessarily mean an entire fantasy planet. For our purposes the world of our campaign is probably just a small handful of countries or kingdoms, and more specifically a few towns and interesting landmarks within those regions.

We'll go around the table each answering a few prompts from the following question lists and sketching representations of our answers onto a map was we go:

Locations

  • What natural landmark inspires awe?
  • What structure is shrouded in mystery?
  • What is the most dangerous town to live in?
  • Where would you travel to find something rare?
  • What place reminds you of the real world?
  • Is this world an unusual shape?
  • What place is untamed by civilisation?
  • Where would someone hide a valuable treasure?
  • Where would a traveller go to relax?
  • What place or thing do people make pilgrimages to visit?
  • Where do people seek knowledge?
  • What's an exciting place by the sea to visit?
  • Where is it treacherous to travel?

People

  • Who is undeniably more powerful than the party?
  • What powerful organisation might your character deal with cautiously?
  • Who does your character respect?
  • If you burned most of your bridges, who would you turn to for help?
  • Who guards secrets that you might want to know?
  • Which organisation is useful enough to make up for being occasionally inconvenient?
  • Who is the most respected leader your character can think of?
  • Who controls a great deal of power behind the scenes?
  • Which antagonistic group is more complicated that it appears?
  • Which organisation supports the arts?
  • What gang, tribe, or faction is just fun to hang out with?

Rumours and Mysteries

  • What threat goes by many names?
  • Which living figure do some believe to be dead?
  • Which dead figure do some believe to be alive?
  • How does the sun rise and fall?
  • What ruin defies explanation?
  • What natural event seems to conflict with our understanding of nature?
  • What lost treasure has claimed the lives of many who sought it?
  • Why do travellers avoid strangers?
  • What happens to items that seem to disappear?
  • Who has been betrayed but does not know it?
  • What great threat waits to be awakened?
  • What skill provides mysterious power?
  • Where does one seek unknowable truth?

Character Creation

We'll roll our character stats together, in person. However, You can do point buy or standard array if you prefer (even after seeing the results of your dice rolls!)

Roll Stats

  • Roll 4d6 and drop the lowest.
    e.g. [3, 5, 1, 4] = 3 + 5 + 4 (dropping the 1) = 12
  • Repeat this process until you have generated six values
  • Assign those values to your character's six primary ability scores
  • Remember to adjust the values for any Race and Class modifiers that may apply
  • Record ability modifiers:
    • A score of 10 or 11 give an ability modifier of 0 (i.e. no modifier)
    • Every even number above 10 grants +1
    • Every odd number below 10 represents -1

Previous Adventures

Write a very short plot summary for an adventure your character previously embarked on, as if it were a comic book or movie (but shorter - a couple of sentences is fine).

  • What was at stake / who or what was threatened?
  • Who was the antagonist(s) / what was the nature of the threat?
  • What was a skilful, cool, or heroic thing your character did during the adventure?
  • Why was the outcome of the adventure both good and bad?

Joint Ventures

Repeat the above steps twice more, each time collaborating with one other player. At the third step both characters should state one heroic thing they did during the adventure.
